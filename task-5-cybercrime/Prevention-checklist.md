Here’s a clear “Stay Safe Online” checklist tailored for college students in India. It includes actionable steps, with two tips specific to UPI/financial safety, and the official reporting channels:
## Stay Safe Online Checklist

### ✅ Do's
- Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts and enable two-factor authentication.  
- Keep your devices and apps updated to patch security vulnerabilities.  
- Verify links and sender details before clicking or responding to emails/messages.  
- Adjust privacy settings on social media to control who can view your posts.  
- Log out of shared or public computers after use.  
- **For UPI safety:** Enter your UPI PIN only when sending money, never to “receive” funds.  
- **For UPI safety:** Double-check the recipient’s name and account before confirming a transaction.  
- Report suspicious activity immediately via the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** or **Helpline 1930**.  

### ❌ Don'ts
- Don’t share personal details (like Aadhaar, bank info, or passwords) on social media.  
- Don’t accept friend requests or messages from unknown accounts.  
- Don’t download apps from unverified sources outside official app stores.  
- Don’t overshare your location or daily routine online.  
- Don’t ignore suspicious activity — act quickly to protect yourself.

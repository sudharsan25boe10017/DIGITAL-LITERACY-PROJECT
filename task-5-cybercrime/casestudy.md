## Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a growing cybercrime in India, targeting unsuspecting users of digital payment platforms. The crime typically involves tricking individuals into authorizing transactions that transfer money directly to the fraudster’s account.  

### How it happens (step-by-step):
- The fraudster contacts the victim, often posing as a buyer or customer service agent.  
- They send a fake UPI “collect request” or QR code, claiming it will credit money to the victim’s account.  
- The victim, believing the request is legitimate, enters their UPI PIN to “receive” money.  
- Instead of receiving funds, the victim unknowingly authorizes a debit transaction.  
- The fraudster immediately withdraws or transfers the stolen amount.  

### Who is targeted:
Students, first-time digital payment users, and elderly individuals are common targets, as they may be less familiar with UPI’s mechanics.  

### Consequences:
Victims often lose significant sums of money, face emotional distress, and may struggle to recover funds due to the speed of digital transfers. In one real incident reported by *The Times of India* (2023), a college student lost ₹25,000 after entering her PIN to “receive” a refund from a fake seller.

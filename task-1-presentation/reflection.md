## Project Report: Digital Literacy Infographic

For my project, I used an AI-powered design tool to create a digital infographic titled **“Digital Literacy for Students.”** The visual layout is divided into five colorful sections, each highlighting a key aspect of digital literacy: understanding what it means, essential tools for students, safe internet practices, building a professional online presence, and email etiquette. Each section includes icons and concise tips to make the information easy to understand and visually engaging.

The design aims to help students quickly grasp the importance of digital skills and how to apply them in academic and personal settings. It’s structured to be used as a classroom poster, study aid, or awareness resource.

One interesting challenge I faced was balancing clarity with visual appeal. I had to decide which icons and tips would be most effective without overcrowding the slide. It was fascinating to see how visual hierarchy—like color coding and icon placement—can guide attention and improve comprehension. This project taught me how design and content work together to communicate ideas effectively.
